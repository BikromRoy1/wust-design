WUST’s Mission

The mission of WUST is to provide a diverse student body with career-related education based on scholarly, innovative, and practical approaches to meet emerging global challenges, through the following objectives:

  • To develop career-related programs and emphasize skill development.
  • To provide practical training through CPT; and
  • To provide students with ongoing career development services

WUST’s Purpose

The purpose of WUST and its programs is to provide affordable yet effective education services with lifelong learning opportunities to every human being for their intellectual, professional, and leadership development and growth, whether they are young or old, rich or poor, privileged or underprivileged (financially, physically and/or socially), local or remote.

WUST Scholarships & Tuition Assistance

  • An applicant must be in good standing (financial, academic, and otherwise) with the university.
  • WUST’s scholarship awards are tuition scholarships only. As such, the amount of the award will be credited against the tuition balance a student has to pay in each term.
  • Scholarship/tuition assistance is awarded after applying for any government or outside institution grant (for example Military benefits, Company scholarships directly awarded to the school) and the total amount of all the financial assistance can’t be over 100% of the tuition of the term.
  • All prospective applicants must meet the admission requirements for the program of study they applied for before being considered for any scholarship award.
  • No student will be awarded two or more types of scholarships/tuition assistance concurrently.
  • Each scholarship recipient is required to maintain the eligibility requirements of the scholarship/tuition assistance awarded to him/her throughout the duration of the study.
  • Students who demonstrate the following issues may lose their scholarship award, regardless of the type of award:
  • WUST’s scholarship programs are open to both domestic and international students.
  • If a current student drops out of a program or changes his/her program of study, the student must re-apply for the scholarship/tuition assistance program upon reinstatement of his/her enrollment with WUST.
  • Each scholarship is awarded for the life of the program unless otherwise specified in advance. Therefore, students are no longer required to renew their scholarship application for each term, unless a student lost eligibility for the current award or plans to apply for another one.
  • Scholarship and Tuition Assistance is for Tuition only and is not applied to any other fees.

Washington University of Science and Technology (WUST) was founded in 2008 with the aim of providing innovative and affordable education to people around the world. Located in Virginia, WUST is certified to operate by the State Council of Higher Education for Virginia (SCHEV) and is accredited by the Accrediting Commission of Career Schools and Colleges (ACCSC) to award degrees in MBA, MSIT, MSCS, BBA, and BSIT.

One of the key objectives of WUST is to equip its students with the skills they need to succeed in the workplace. To achieve this, WUST places great emphasis on career placement services and offers academic knowledge alongside technical, soft, and personal skills training. The university's staff works tirelessly to ensure that every graduate finds a job.

WUST has a strong commitment to bridging the gap between academia and industry. To this end, it only hires faculty members who have a minimum of four years' experience in their respective fields. The faculty at WUST are scholar-practitioners whose contributions are crucial to helping students develop the right set of technical and soft skills for employment.

WUST is recognized by the US Department of Education and the Immigration and Customs Enforcement (ICE) to enroll nonimmigrant (F-1) students. Additionally, it is approved by the US Department of Education to participate in the Federal Student Financial Aid program, making career-related professional development opportunities affordable for people from all walks of life.

The university's success in creating an ecosystem between university and industry has resulted in the production of a domestic skilled workforce to meet the demands of the industry. WUST's commitment to producing highly skilled graduates is especially important given the increasing reliance on the H1B program, which has become a national security matter.

WUST's dedication to creating opportunities for its students is reflected in its faculty and staff, who are committed to providing personalized care and attention to each student. The university encourages its students to make a personal commitment to achieving professional growth and securing a decent job.

Overall, WUST has achieved remarkable success in a relatively short period of time. Its commitment to providing innovative and affordable education, bridging the gap between academia and industry, and producing a highly skilled domestic workforce has made it a highly respected institution in the field of education. Its Chairman and Chancellor, Abubokor Hanip, is a highly successful education entrepreneur and IT enthusiast who has made significant contributions to the field of education both in the America and also in the South-Asia.

IGU and AAU will explore and pursue many areas of academic partnership and collaborations, including, but not limited to, the following activities:

  • IGU and AAU will develop "1+3," "2+2," and "3+1" years for bachelor’s degree programs (BSIT and BBA) and "1+1" year for master's degree programs (MSIT and MBA) for AAU students to pursue accredited U.S. degrees at IGU. IGU and AAU will conduct course-by-course evaluations per program to determine the exact length of AAU student’s academic program at IGU.
  • IGU and AAU agree to share all the student records, including degrees and academic grades, of each AAU student who graduates from IGU, so that AAU may consider awarding an AAU degree comparable to the IGU degree earned at IGU. As a result of this arrangement, the AAU student will receive a degree from both IGU and AAU and become an alumnus of both IGU and AAU. This special arrangement may be referred to as a “double degree program.”
  • IGU and AAU will help AAU students start taking IGU courses online in Jordan before they are transferred to an IGU Program.

J-1 Student

Students can enroll in a degree or non-degree-seeking program (cannot enroll in English language learning programs). The J-1 student must be proficient in English and must meet all admissions requirements for a specific program. In its majority (51%), the student's funding source comes from a source other than personal (i.e., scholarship, grant, employer). On a J-1 visa, students can participate in Academic Training during their studies which is authorized part-time employment. The Academic Training for undergraduate and pre-doctoral training does not exceed 18 months, inclusive of any prior academic training in the United States, or the period of a full course of study in the United States, whichever is less; except that additional time for academic training is allowed to the extent necessary for the exchange visitor to satisfy the mandatory requirements of his or her degree program in the United States. Academic training must be directly related to the student's field of study. Additionally, student spouses on a J-2 visa are eligible to request employment authorization.

J-1 Student Intern

This program allows the student to come to the U.S.A. to participate in an internship program at W.U.S.T. or at another company (approved by W.U.S.T.). The participants must be currently enrolled in a degree-seeking program in their home country (not graduated). They must plan to return home after the internship to complete their program of study and graduate. The internship field must be full-time and must match the student's field of study in his/her home country. The internships can be paid or unpaid and have a maximum duration of 12 months.

J-1 Professor

Foreign professors can apply to participate as visiting professors at W.U.S.T. The program duration is 3 or 6 months and can be extended if the W.U.S.T. and the visiting professor deem it necessary. Visiting professor positions are subject to need and availability. Duties can include teaching, co-teaching, leading workshops, guest speaking, conducting research, curriculum development, etc. W.U.S.T. will provide a monthly stipend to pay for accommodation and living expenses in the U.S.A.
